Facilitator Interns – RODI Recruitment

RODI is seeking to recruit 3 Facilitator Interns for a period of 12 months to collaborate with the site coordinator and engage with young students (peer educators) and youth to organize community events and campus workshops, adhering to the organization’s plan, budget, and timeline.

The intern will participate in planning sessions to develop and review skills training and delivery, undertaking various duties, responsibilities, and qualities essential for success in this role.

2. Key Responsibilities

1. Role-Modelling

As key message-givers to the Peer Educators, Facilitator Interns are required to model health-enhancing and purpose-driven behaviour. Their attitudes and behaviour directly impact the Peer Educators they work with. Interns should project personal integrity and a commitment to empowering others.

2. Educating

  • Plan, deliver, and review regular skills training sessions to Peer Educators in schools and community sites.
  • Assist Peer Educators to achieve their service and output targets.
  • Maintain regular contact with Peer Educators and follow up on non-attendance or lack of participation.

3. Mentoring

The Facilitator Intern should mentor Peer Educators both formally and informally, one-on-one and in groups. Ongoing training and support will be provided to meet this requirement.

4. Counselling

Provide time and space for Peer Educators in need of personal support. Interns will receive supervision and support through team meetings and case-management sessions.

5. Project Management

  • Organize community events, workshops, or camps according to RODI’s annual plan, program, and budget.
  • Participate in planning and reviewing skills training and delivery sessions.
  • Support program delivery through skills training, academic support, workshops, community projects, advocacy, and related activities.

6. Stakeholder Management

Engage and build relationships with community stakeholders, including teachers, parents, youth leaders, health workers, and community leaders. This will focus on youth risk behaviour reduction, education support, and employability development.

7. Administration

  • Maintain and update registers, training files, and program documentation within deadlines.
  • Record all planning sessions, reviews, and implemented activities as required.

3. Required Attitudes

  • Ability to engage with community stakeholders, school, and government leaders.
  • Strong communication skills with both adults and youth.
  • Knowledge of youth behaviour change, education support, and employability development.
  • Understanding of HIV/AIDS, sexual and reproductive health, and life-skills issues.
  • Strong leadership skills.
  • Experience in administration and report writing.
  • Ability to train and facilitate in both small and large groups.
  • Teamwork and participative collaboration skills.
  • Counselling skills (advantageous).
  • Basic computer literacy.
  • Passion for youth empowerment and positive role-modelling.
  • Strong personal values and character.

4. Qualifications and Experience

  • Aged between 18–24 years old.
  • Minimum of Senior Six, A1, or a Bachelor’s Degree in social sciences, education, or related fields.
  • Skills in using technology tools such as computers, tablets, and smartphones.
  • Training or experience in basic counselling.
  • Experience in working or volunteering with youth.
  • Experience or training in youth life-skills is preferred.
  • Unemployed but actively involved in community service (preferred).
  • Any specific talents to engage youth can strengthen the application.

5. Work Location and Collaboration

The Facilitator Interns will be based in Ruhango District at RODI’s sub-office. Preference will be given to applicants residing near Byimana and Ruhango Sectors for optimal program performance.

Interns will collaborate closely with the RODI team, youth, school administrators, local leaders, and community members.
